Game Features & Mechanics

Physics-Based Destruction System

Realistic falling sand cellular automata powers every interaction. Materials behave according to natural physics laws, creating authentic destruction patterns that respond to force, momentum, and environmental conditions in believable ways.

Heat Transfer Chain Reactions

Advanced temperature mechanics create cascading destruction effects. Shrapnel generates heat based on velocity, stone melts at specific thresholds, and excess heat transfers to adjacent materials, triggering secondary explosions.

Procedural Building Generation

Sophisticated algorithms create unique building structures for every contract. Geometric patterns combine rectangles and detail lines to generate everything from small workshops to complex multi-story structures, all properly grounded.

Material Property System

Four distinct materials each behave differently: durable terrain that resists damage, explosive stone that transfers heat, high-velocity shrapnel that penetrates and spreads heat, and inert ash that settles naturally under gravity.

Momentum-Based Shrapnel

Shrapnel velocity directly affects penetration damage and heat distribution. Faster-moving fragments pierce deeper and transfer more thermal energy, while air resistance gradually slows particles, creating realistic ballistic behavior.

Power Scaling Mechanics

Hold-and-release charging system scales both blast radius and destructive force. Longer charges create larger destruction zones with more intense effects, allowing strategic choices between precision strikes and overwhelming force.
