Orbital City Buster

Sand Breaker Ball

Control an experimental paddle warship from orbit and demolish cities below using physics-based projectiles. City Buster combines the precision gameplay of Breakout with the satisfying destruction mechanics of falling sand simulations, creating a unique orbital bombardment experience where every impact creates cascading destruction through realistic material physics.

Launch energy orbs downward to shatter terrain, melt materials, and trigger chain reactions that spread fire and force throughout procedurally generated cities. Each collision transforms the environment - wood burns to ash, sand melts to glass, concrete shatters to aggregate - creating emergent destruction patterns that are never the same twice.

Your goal is to locate and destroy hidden objectives buried within these physics-simulated cities while dodging retaliatory projectiles and collecting power-ups that enhance your destructive capabilities. The faster and more precisely you strike, the more devastating your area-of-effect becomes.

Lore & Story

The Helios Sector sprawls across fifty star systems in humanity's newest colonial frontier, where the promise of new worlds has given way to lawlessness and corruption. What began as mankind's greatest expansion has devolved into a contested region where corrupt colonial leaders have transformed into mafia bosses, seizing control of the means of production across multiple exoplanets.


In the sector's early days, legitimate colonial governments established mining operations, manufacturing centers, and agricultural settlements across dozens of worlds. But as Earth's oversight weakened over the vast distances, local mayors and administrators began forming criminal networks, exploiting workers, stealing resources, and building private armies to protect their illicit operations.


Among those affected was a local weapon research group whose experimental technologies were seized by these corrupt forces. Rather than submit to the growing tyranny, the research team formed a vigilante organization, using their most advanced creation: the Paddle Ship orbital bombardment system. This experimental weapon platform combines precision targeting with devastating physics-based destruction capabilities.


Your mission as a reckless renegade for hire is to systematically dismantle these criminal networks by targeting their infrastructure, supply chains, and power bases. Sponsored by an unhinged religious cult that views the mafia leaders as an affront to cosmic order, you operate with complete tactical freedom and minimal concern for collateral damage.


The goal is liberation through destruction - methodically eliminating the corrupt organizations that have poisoned the Helios Sector, one campaign at a time, until legitimate governance can be restored to these fifty star systems. Each successful mission weakens the criminal stranglehold and brings hope to the oppressed populations below.

Game Layers

Moment-to-Moment Active Gameplay

Players control their orbital paddle ship to launch and redirect energy orbs into procedurally generated cities. Each projectile impact creates physics-based destruction: shattering concrete into falling aggregate, melting sand into molten glass, igniting wooden structures that spread fire to surrounding materials. Players must simultaneously manage multiple projectiles, dodge retaliatory fire from destroyed objectives, and collect power-ups while maintaining orbs within the play area through precise paddle positioning and timing.

Low Metagame

Nine-stage campaigns target specific criminal organizations across the Helios Sector. Each campaign introduces a mafia gang with unique characteristics, crimes, and difficulty modifiers that affect terrain generation, hazard density, and power-up availability. Players progress through increasingly complex cities, each procedurally generated with embedded objectives to destroy. Campaign completion is tracked with performance metrics, and individual stages can be replayed using their generation seeds for practice or high-score attempts.

High Metagame

The persistent starmap of the Helios Sector presents three difficulty-tiered campaign options at any time: easy (1 crime), medium (2-3 crimes), or chaotic (4-5 crimes). Completed campaigns are permanently logged as "memory flashbacks" that can be revisited, while unchosen campaigns are handled by other vigilantes, maintaining the sense of an ongoing galactic conflict. Each campaign may feature unique environmental modifiers (different gravity, enhanced explosions, material behavior changes) that create distinct tactical challenges and require players to adapt their destruction strategies.

Practical Game Flow

Players launch the game, select from available campaigns based on desired time commitment and difficulty preference, then engage in individual destruction stages that typically last 5-15 minutes. Natural stopping points occur between stages and campaigns, with progress automatically saved. The physics simulation provides immediate satisfaction while the campaign structure offers long-term progression goals, allowing players to engage in quick destructive sessions or extended tactical campaigns based on available time and energy.

Game Features & Mechanics

üß™ Falling Sand Destruction Physics

Advanced material simulation system where each pixel behaves according to realistic physics properties. Wood burns and spreads fire, sand melts into glass under heat, concrete shatters into aggregate, water becomes steam, and molten materials cool into ash over time. Materials interact through heat convection, kinetic force, and chemical transformation, creating emergent destruction patterns where no two impacts produce identical results. The simulation includes 15+ material types, each with unique properties for burning, melting, shattering, and transforming, enabling complex chain reactions that spread destruction organically throughout the environment.

üéØ Dynamic Ammo Types & Upgrade System

Five distinct ammunition types offer varied tactical approaches: Normal (reliable medium impact), Triple (widespread light damage), Accelerator (building velocity), Splitter (cascading fragmentation), and Charge (escalating power). Power-ups collected during gameplay stack to enhance ammunition effects, including increased blast radius, heat generation, piercing capabilities, and mass for greater kinetic impact. Paddle ship upgrades provide magnetic catchers, gravity fields, extended flight zones, and defensive capabilities, creating a progression system that rewards both skilled play and tactical power-up management.

üåç Procedural Terrain & Campaign Generation

Sophisticated terrain generation creates unique cities using layered material placement (grass/wood/water surface, sand/soil middle, stone/bedrock foundation) with embedded structures, tunnels, and objectives. Seed-based generation ensures infinite variety while maintaining balanced, playable layouts. Each campaign features distinct environmental characteristics influenced by the target organization's profile, including material distribution, objective placement, and hazard density. Generated cities include surface buildings, underground facilities, suspended platforms, and hidden chambers that require strategic excavation to access.

‚öîÔ∏è Risk/Reward Hazard Management

Destroyed objectives launch retaliatory projectiles (fireballs, lightning, shrapnel) that threaten the paddle ship while potentially dropping valuable power-ups. Players must balance aggressive destruction with defensive positioning, using the slow-motion ability to navigate dense hazard patterns while collecting upgrades. Each hit reduces paddle ship armor, but power-ups can restore health and provide temporary shielding, creating dynamic risk assessment scenarios where players choose between safe, distant bombardment and dangerous close-quarters collection runs.

üî• Material Interaction Chemistry

Complex material interaction system governs how destruction spreads through the environment. Heat-based interactions cause materials to ignite, melt, or transform (sand becomes glass, wood becomes fire and ash, concrete becomes lava then cools to slag). Kinetic force interactions shatter materials and propel debris, creating secondary impacts that extend destruction beyond the initial blast zone. Water extinguishes fires but becomes steam under heat, creating dynamic environmental changes that affect both destruction patterns and player visibility during intense bombardment sequences.

‚ôæÔ∏è Infinite Replayability Through Seeded Content

Every campaign and individual stage uses procedural generation with recorded seeds, enabling perfect reproduction of any previously played scenario. The campaign selection system continuously generates fresh challenges while maintaining a complete log of conquered territories for replay. Environmental modifiers (gravity variations, material behavior changes, explosion multipliers) ensure each campaign feels distinctly different, while the physics simulation guarantees that even replayed stages offer unique destruction experiences based on player choices and timing variations.

Game Modes

Campaign Mode

Primary Experience: Structured progression through procedurally generated nine-stage campaigns targeting criminal organizations. Each campaign provides 1-2 hours of focused destruction with escalating difficulty, unique environmental modifiers, and narrative context. Players choose from three available difficulty tiers, with completed campaigns recorded for future replay. This mode balances immediate destruction satisfaction with long-term progression goals, offering natural stopping points between stages while maintaining campaign momentum.

Memory Flashback Mode

Replay & Mastery: Access to all previously completed stages and campaigns using their original generation seeds. Players can revisit favorite destruction scenarios, practice difficult stages, or attempt high-score runs with knowledge of objective locations and optimal strategies. This mode serves both nostalgia and skill development, allowing players to experiment with different ammunition types and tactical approaches on familiar terrain while experiencing the unique physics variations that make each playthrough distinct despite identical starting conditions.
