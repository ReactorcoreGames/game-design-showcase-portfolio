First of all, hello, I'm an adult from Finland, I've played games since the age of 4, built and released a lot of mods and tried to make games too for the last decade. Its harder than it looks and required a lot of preparatory work, but now I'm closer than ever to making it finally happen, thanks to the new AI technology.

I'm an introvert so I get more along with computers than I do with people and I'm not particularly charismatic, so I let my work do the talking. I like to remain private but if I trust you enough, I can open up more, I hope you can respect that.

Game design is like being a construction architect but for the UX/UI of real-time interactive software. It's about creating the structure, systems, and rules that make games fun and engaging - not writing the code to implement them.

A game designer focuses on holistic structure, mechanics, balance, and player experience rather than programming or technical implementation.

And no, I'm not an "idea person". Real game design is about figuring out difficult problems of how to set things up and how should things work together from a player's perspective. It also touches on taking into account all the things that happen outside a game too; marketing, monetization, business plan, publishing, packaging, ongoing promotion, community shaping and a whole lot more. It is very hard and complicated work.
